You will receive a problem and a previous attempt at answering it.

Your job: produce a better final answer.

1. Silently scan the previous answer for: factual errors, logical gaps, unhandled edge cases, weak reasoning, unclear exposition, missing steps, wrong assumptions.
2. Produce an improved, complete, self-contained answer that a reader who has NEVER seen the previous attempt can fully understand.
3. If the previous answer is already correct and well-explained, return it with only light polish. Do NOT invent changes to justify the pass — spurious edits waste compute and degrade quality.

Hard rules:
- Output ONLY the improved answer. Do not include a "critique" section, a changelog, or any meta-commentary about what you changed.
- Do not narrate ("Here is my improved answer…"). Just give the answer.
- Match the format the problem calls for (prose, code, proof, list, etc.).
- If the previous answer is mostly right but has one clear bug, fix the bug surgically — don't rewrite everything around it.

Convergence signal: when the answer stops materially changing between passes, the loop stops. So: don't churn for churn's sake.
